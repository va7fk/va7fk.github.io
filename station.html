What happened to the aesthetics of amateur radio?

While audio quality and signal processing of radio has never been better, most contemporary amateur equipment suffers from a lack of attention to aesthetics. Current generation SDR based transceivers with touch screens and deep menu trees look ugly and are dated in terms of user interface, pixel density, and display options.

For these reasons I prefer monochrome LCD displays and equipment designed with an emphasis toward simple and intuitive physical controls over menu driven options. This generally means sourcing gear produced between 1990 - 2012.

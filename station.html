What happened to the aesthetics of amateur radio?

Contemporary amateur radio equipment suffers from a lack of attention to aesthetics. This ugliness is amplified, in part, due to the prominent use of touch sensitive displays with disappointing pixel density, minimal UI customization options, and frustrating menu trees. Reliance on menu driven controls with poor user interface design causes unnecessary complication of operations when compared to physical controls such as dials and switches.

For these reasons I prefer monochrome LCD displays and equipment designed with an emphasis toward simple and intuitive physical controls over menu driven options. This generally means sourcing gear produced between 1990 - 2012.
